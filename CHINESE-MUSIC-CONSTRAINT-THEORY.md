# Chinese Music Theory as Constraint-Theory Foundation

**A cross-cultural mathematical investigation connecting 五音, 十二律, and 古琴 to Eisenstein lattices, Laman rigidity, and deadband funnels.**

---

## 0. Motivation

Western music theory assumes 12-tone equal temperament (12-TET) as its "natural" substrate. It isn't — it's a historical compromise. Chinese music theory developed sophisticated pitch, scale, and constraint systems independently, starting ~2500 years before Europe standardized on 12-TET. These systems have fundamentally different mathematical structures that may offer richer constraint-theory primitives.

**The central question:** Can Chinese music theory provide an alternative (or complementary) mathematical foundation for constraint-theory music that is *at least as rigorous* as the Eisenstein A₂ lattice, and potentially more expressive?

## 1. 五音 (Wǔyīn) — The Five-Tone System

### 1.1 The Pentatonic Scale as a Mathematical Object

The Chinese pentatonic scale consists of five tones:

| Tone | Pinyin | Character | Solfege | Interval from Gōng |
|------|--------|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| 宮   | gōng   | 宮        | do      | 0 (unison)          |
| 商   | shāng  | 商        | re      | 2 semitones         |
| 角   | jué    | 角        | mi      | 4 semitones         |
| 徵   | zhǐ    | 徵        | sol     | 7 semitones         |
| 羽   | yǔ     | 羽        | la      | 9 semitones         |

The intervals are: {0, 2, 4, 7, 9} modulo 12.

### 1.2 Group-Theoretic Structure

In 12-TET (Z/12Z, +), the pentatonic scale generates a **subgroup**:

- The generating set {2, 4, 7, 9} has GCD = gcd(2, 4, 7, 9) = 1, so the subgroup generated is **all of Z/12Z**.
- However, the pentatonic scale as a *set* is not a subgroup — it's a **coset** of a subgroup.
- The relevant subgroup is ⟨2⟩ = {0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10} (whole-tone scale), of order 6.
- The pentatonic set {0, 2, 4, 7, 9} is NOT a coset of ⟨2⟩ — it contains both even (0,2,4) and odd (7,9) elements.

**Correct analysis:** The pentatonic scale is generated by stacking **perfect fifths** (7 semitones):

```
C → G → D → A → E → (B, skip) → ...
```

Taking five consecutive fifths and reducing mod 12:
- 0, 7, 14≡2, 21≡9, 28≡4 → **{0, 2, 4, 7, 9}** ✓

This means the pentatonic scale is the **orbit of 0 under repeated application of T₇** (transposition by fifth), truncated at 5 elements before the cycle would close.

**The key group-theoretic fact:** In Z/12Z, ⟨7⟩ = Z/12Z (since gcd(7,12) = 1). The pentatonic is a *partial* fifth-chain — it's what you get by stopping the circle of fifths at 5 instead of 12. It's a 5-element subset closed under *some* operations but not all.

### 1.3 The Pentatonic as a 5-Cycle

The five tones in fifth-order are: 宮→徵→商→羽→角→(back to 宫+comma)

This is a **5-cycle** within Z/12Z. The structure is:

```
S₅ = {0, 7, 2, 9, 4} (fifth-chain order)
```

In fifth-space (where the unit = perfect fifth = 7 semitones), this maps to **Z/5Z** — a clean cyclic group. The pentatonic is naturally a Z/5Z structure embedded in Z/12Z via the homomorphism n ↦ 7n mod 12.

This is **profound**: pentatonic music lives in a 5-dimensional cyclic world that only imperfectly maps to 12-TET. The "imperfection" is exactly the Pythagorean comma.

### 1.4 A₂ Lattice Under 5-Tone vs 7-Tone Restriction

The A₂ Eisenstein lattice in `constraint_theory_core/lattice.py` operates in 12-TET space. Its covering radius ρ = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 guarantees any pitch-class point snaps to a lattice point.

**Restricting to 5-tone (pentatonic):**

When we restrict the lattice to only accept snaps in {0, 2, 4, 7, 9}, the effective lattice becomes **sparser**. The covering radius increases because there are fewer target points.

For a pentatonic subset of Z/12Z, the maximum gap between consecutive tones is:
- Between 角(4) and 徵(7): gap = 3 semitones
- Between 羽(9) and 宮(12≡0): gap = 3 semitones
- All other gaps = 2 semitones

The worst-case covering in semitone space is 1.5 semitones (half of max gap 3), vs 0.5 semitones for the full chromatic.

**In Eisenstein coordinates:** The A₂ lattice has basis {1, ω}. Restricting to 5 out of 12 pitch classes means only 5/12 of the lattice points are "valid." The effective covering radius scales by approximately √(12/5) ≈ 1.549, giving ρ₅ ≈ 0.577 × 1.549 ≈ 0.894.

**This means pentatonic constraint resolution is inherently less precise but more forgiving** — notes can wander further before snapping to a valid tone. This matches the aesthetic: pentatonic music is *more ambiguous* than diatonic music, and this ambiguity has a precise mathematical measure.

**Restricting to 7-tone (heptatonic with 变宫/变徵):**

Adding 變宮 (biàngōng, ≈ti) and 變徵 (biànzhǐ, ≈fa) gives a 7-note scale. The Chinese heptatonic is not identical to the Western major — it's derived from the same fifth-chain, yielding modes:

```
{0, 2, 4, 7, 9} + {6, 11} or {5, 10} depending on the 變 (altered) tones
```

With 7 tones, max gap = 2 semitones (only the minor-second gaps remain), and the covering radius approaches the full chromatic value. **Heptatonic Chinese is nearly as constraint-tight as Western diatonic**, which explains why Chinese heptatonic and Western major/minor can blend relatively easily.

### 1.5 五行 (Wǔxíng) Constraint System

The five tones map to the five phases (五行):

| Tone   | Phase | Chinese | Element |
|--------|-------|---------|---------|
| 宮 gōng| Earth | 土      | Central, grounding |
| 商 shāng| Metal | 金      | Clear, cutting |
| 角 jué | Wood  | 木      | Growing, bending |
| 徵 zhǐ | Fire  | 火      | Rising, bright |
| 羽 yǔ  | Water | 水      | Flowing, descending |

**Two fundamental cycles:**

#### Generating Cycle (相生 xiāngshēng): Wood→Fire→Earth→Metal→Water→Wood

```
角(jué/Wood) → 徵(zhǐ/Fire) → 宮(gōng/Earth) → 商(shāng/Metal) → 羽(yǔ/Water) → 角
```

In fifth-space: {4, 7→0→2→9} = continuing the fifth-chain. **The generating cycle IS the circle of fifths in pentatonic space.** This is not metaphor — it's mathematically identical to stacking T₇ in Z/12Z restricted to 5 elements.

#### Overcoming Cycle (相剋 xiāngkè): Wood→Earth→Water→Fire→Metal→Wood

```
角(jué/Wood) → 宮(gōng/Earth) → 羽(yǔ/Water) → 徵(zhǐ/Fire) → 商(shāng/Metal) → 角
```

In fifth-space, this skips every other element — it's the **T₁₄ ≡ T₂** operation in Z/12Z, i.e., transposition by major second. The overcoming cycle is the pentatonic traversed in whole-tone steps.

### 1.6 Wǔxíng as Directed Graph → Laman Rigidity

The wǔxíng system defines a **directed graph** on 5 vertices with two edge sets:

- **Generating edges (xiāngshēng):** 5 directed edges forming a 5-cycle (Z₅)
- **Overcoming edges (xiāngkè):** 5 directed edges forming a different 5-cycle (Z₅ with skip-2)

**Laman rigidity on K₅:** The complete graph K₅ has |E| = 10 edges. For Laman rigidity on 5 vertices in 2D, we need exactly 2n-3 = 7 edges with the Laman condition (every subset of k vertices spans at most 2k-3 edges).

The wǔxíng graph has 10 edges (both cycles). This is 3 more than needed for Laman rigidity — the system is **over-constrained** by 3 degrees of freedom. This means:

1. The pentatonic system is **rigid** (no non-trivial motions preserve the constraints)
2. It has **redundancy** — removing up to 3 edges still leaves a rigid system
3. The three "extra" edges provide **self-checking** — a broken constraint doesn't collapse the system

**This is a profound insight:** Chinese music theory encodes a self-repairing constraint system. The two cycles (generating and overcoming) provide redundant constraints that make the tonal system robust against perturbation. If one relationship is weakened, the system doesn't collapse — the other cycle holds it together.

In Laman terms: the generating cycle alone (5 edges on 5 vertices, needing 7 for rigidity) is **flexible** — it has 2 internal degrees of freedom. Adding the overcoming cycle (5 more edges) over-constrains by 3, making the system **super-stable**. This maps to the Chinese philosophical concept that 相生 and 相剋 are both necessary — one cycle alone is unstable, both together create a balanced, self-correcting system.

**Constraint-theory implication:** A Laman-rigid pentatonic constraint engine would:
- Have 5 pitch-class vertices
- Enforce interval constraints from both xiāngshēng and xiāngkè
- Allow 0 degrees of freedom (fully determined)
- Tolerate removal of up to 3 constraints without collapse
- Self-repair by checking which cycle's constraints are violated

## 2. 十二律 (Shí'èrlǜ) — The Twelve Pitches

### 2.1 Historical Context

China had a 12-pitch system documented by the 3rd century BCE (Lüshi Chunqiu, 管子 Guanzi). Each pitch has a name:

| # | Name   | Pinyin      | Approx. ratio from fundamental |
|---|--------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | 黄钟   | Huángzhōng  | 1/1                           |
| 2 | 大吕   | Dàlǚ        | 2187/2048                     |
| 3 | 太簇   | Tàicù       | 9/8                           |
| 4 | 夹钟   | Jiāzhōng    | 19683/16384                   |
| 5 | 姑洗   | Gūxǐ        | 81/64                         |
| 6 | 仲吕   | Zhònglǚ     | 177147/131072                 |
| 7 | 蕤宾   | Ruíbīn      | 729/512                       |
| 8 | 林钟   | Línzhōng    | 3/2                           |
| 9 | 夷则   | Yízé        | 6561/4096                     |
|10 | 南吕   | Nánlǚ       | 27/16                         |
|11 | 无射   | Wúyì        | 59049/32768                   |
|12 | 应钟   | Yìngzhōng   | 243/128                       |

### 2.2 三分损益 (Sānfēn Sǔnyì) — The Generating Algorithm

The algorithm is:
1. Start with a fundamental length L
2. **三分损一** (reduce by 1/3): L × 2/3 → pitch goes UP a fifth
3. **三分益一** (add 1/3): L × 4/3 → pitch goes DOWN a fourth
4. Alternate these operations to generate all 12 pitches

This produces the **Pythagorean spiral of fifths**:

```
L₀ × (2/3) × (4/3) × (2/3) × (4/3) × ...
= L₀ × (2/3)^n × (4/3)^m  where n+m = total steps
```

After 12 steps, the spiral returns *approximately* to the starting pitch but off by the **Pythagorean comma**:

```
(3/2)¹² = 1297463379 / 531441 ≈ 531441.094...
vs 2⁷ = 128

Ratio = 531441/524288 ≈ 1.01364... ≈ 23.46 cents
```

This is the **Pythagorean comma** (逗号), known in Chinese as the **古音差** (gǔyīnchā, "ancient pitch discrepancy").

### 2.3 The Spiral as a Lattice

The 三分损益 system generates a **spiral**, not a circle. In mathematical terms:

- The fifths form a **helix** in pitch space: fₙ = f₀ × (3/2)ⁿ
- After 12 steps, the spiral doesn't close — it's offset by the comma
- The spiral extends infinitely, generating pitch classes that slowly drift

**In lattice terms:** This is NOT the A₂ Eisenstein lattice. The A₂ lattice is *flat* — it lives in Z/12Z where 12 fifths = 7 octaves exactly. The Chinese system lives in an **infinite spiral** where this identity fails.

The pitch-class space is **R/Z** (continuous circle), and the fifths map to rotation by α where:

```
α = log₂(3/2) ≈ 0.58496... (in octaves)
```

Since α is **irrational** (because log₂(3) is irrational by the Gelfond-Schneider theorem), the orbit {nα mod 1 : n ∈ Z} is **dense** in the circle. The 12 pitches are just the first 12 points of this dense orbit.

### 2.4 Covering Radius of the Spiral Lattice

The A₂ Eisenstein lattice has covering radius ρ = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 semitones. What's the analogous quantity for the 三分损益 spiral?

After 12 fifths, the comma is 23.46 cents ≈ 0.1955 semitones. This means:
- The 12-tone spiral "almost" closes, with a gap of ~0.2 semitones
- The 12 pitch classes are *nearly* evenly spaced, but not exactly
- The worst-case deviation from equal spacing is the comma distributed across 12 steps: ~23.46/12 ≈ 1.95 cents per step

**The covering radius of the 三分损益 spiral lattice:**

For the first 12 pitches, the maximum gap between consecutive pitch classes (in the circle ordering) varies. In the Pythagorean tuning, some fifths are pure (701.96¢) and some are "wolf" fifths, narrowed by the comma. The wolf fifth is:

```
701.96 - 23.46 = 678.50¢ ≈ 11.30 semitones (in terms of the fifth)
```

In pitch-class space, the largest gap between consecutive Pythagorean tones is approximately:

```
max gap ≈ 112 cents ≈ 0.933 semitones (between certain semitone intervals)
```

So the covering radius is approximately:
```
ρ_十二律 ≈ 56 cents ≈ 0.467 semitones
```

Compared to A₂: ρ_A₂ = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 semitones.

**The Chinese Pythagorean system actually has a *tighter* covering radius than the A₂ lattice!** This means 三分损益 is slightly more precise in snapping arbitrary pitches to the nearest scale tone — at the cost of not being a closed group (the spiral never exactly closes).

### 2.5 Beyond 12: The Full Spiral

The spiral doesn't stop at 12. Extended Pythagorean systems use 17, 19, 24, 31, 41, 53, or even 84 tones. Each closure gets closer:

- 12 tones: comma = 23.46¢
- 41 tones: comma ≈ 0.48¢ (below JND)
- 53 tones: comma ≈ 3.62¢ (excellent fifth)

The **53-tone Pythagorean system** is remarkably close to equal temperament (53-TET has a fifth of 701.89¢ vs pure 701.96¢). China never developed this far (the practical guqin only needs continuous pitch, not discrete subdivisions), but it's implicit in the mathematics.

**For constraint-theory:** The spiral can be truncated at any level of precision. A constraint engine using 三分损益 could choose its covering radius by choosing how many spiral steps to include — a tunable precision parameter that the A₂ lattice doesn't offer.

## 3. 古琴 (Gǔqín) — The Zither as Constraint System

### 3.1 Physical Setup

The guqin has 7 strings (historically 5), tuned to various pentatonic configurations:
- Standard tuning: C-D-F-G-A-c-d (pentatonic across two octaves)
- No frets, no fixed pitch positions
- The player presses the string against the non-fretted fingerboard to change pitch
- Pitch depends on **where** along the string length the finger is placed

### 3.2 Continuous Pitch: No snap() in the Western Sense

Western `snap()` quantizes continuous pitch to discrete semitones on the A₂ lattice. The guqin has **no discrete pitch grid** — the string is continuous.

But there ARE natural attractors:

#### Open strings (散音 sànyīn)
The fundamental frequency of each string — 7 discrete pitches (or 5 in older instruments).

#### Stopped tones (按音 ànyīn)
The string pressed at any point along its length produces a continuous range of pitches. The "natural" stopping points correspond to **harmonic ratios**:

| Position (fraction of string) | Ratio | Interval | Traditional name |
|-------------------------------|-------|----------|------------------|
| 1/2                           | 2:1   | Octave   | —                |
| 1/3                           | 3:1   | Octave+fifth | 徽位 (huīwèi)  |
| 2/3                           | 3:2   | Fifth    | —                |
| 1/4                           | 4:1   | 2 octaves| —                |
| 3/4                           | 4:3   | Fourth   | —                |
| 1/5                           | 5:1   | 2 oct+M3 | —                |
| 2/5                           | 5:2   | M3+oct   | —                |
| 3/5                           | 5:3   | M6       | —                |
| 4/5                           | 5:4   | M3       | —                |

#### Harmonics (泛音 fànyīn)
The guqin has 13 inlaid markers (徽 huī) on the fingerboard indicating harmonic nodes:

```
huī positions: 1/8, 1/7, 1/6, 1/5, 1/4, 2/7, 1/3, 2/5, 1/2, 3/5, 2/3, 5/7, 3/4, ...
```

(Traditional: 13 markers at specific harmonic positions)

**These harmonic positions form a subset of Q ∩ [0,1]** — the rational numbers between 0 and 1 with small denominators. This is the **harmonic lattice** in just intonation, which is a *completely different structure* from the equal-tempered A₂ lattice.

### 3.3 snap() on a Guqin Lattice

For a guqin-based `snap()`, the target lattice is not Z/12Z but the set of **just intonation ratios**:

```
J = { m/n : m,n ∈ Z⁺, n ≤ N_max }
```

where N_max determines the maximum harmonic complexity allowed.

The covering radius of J depends on N_max:
- N_max = 1: only open strings → ρ ≈ several semitones
- N_max = 2: open + octave → ρ ≈ 3 semitones
- N_max = 5: major triad ratios → ρ ≈ 0.5 semitones
- N_max = 13 (all huī positions): ρ ≈ 0.1 semitones

**A guqin snap() would look like:**

```python
def guqin_snap(frequency: float, fundamental: float, max_denominator: int = 13) -> Tuple[float, float]:
    """Snap a frequency to the nearest just-intonation ratio.
    
    Returns (snapped_freq, error_in_cents).
    """
    ratio = frequency / fundamental
    # Find best rational approximation with bounded denominator
    best_ratio = None
    best_error = float('inf')
    for n in range(1, max_denominator + 1):
        for m in range(n, n * 4):  # up to 4 octaves
            r = m / n
            error_cents = abs(1200 * math.log2(ratio / r))
            if error_cents < best_error:
                best_error = error_cents
                best_ratio = r
    return fundamental * best_ratio, best_error
```

**Key difference from A₂ snap():** The guqin lattice is **non-uniform** — some regions are dense (near simple ratios) and some are sparse. The covering radius varies across pitch space. This is more musically expressive: "easy" intervals (octave, fifth) have tight constraints, while "difficult" intervals (tritones) have wide deadbands.

### 3.4 The Guqin as Continuous Constraint Satisfaction

Western music: compose on a discrete grid → perform with micro-variations (deadband).
Guqin music: compose as continuous constraints → perform by satisfying them in real-time.

The guqin notation system (减字谱 jiǎnzìpǔ) doesn't specify pitches — it specifies **actions**:

```
"Use ring finger, string 3, position 5, pluck with thumb inward"
```

The musician must **infer the pitch** from:
1. Which string (known fundamental)
2. Where to stop it (known position → ratio → pitch)
3. How to pluck (affects timbre, not pitch)
4. Context (what came before, what comes after)

This is **constraint propagation in real-time**. The notation is the constraint graph; the performance is the solver.

## 4. Chinese Notation Systems as Constraint Encodings

### 4.1 简谱 (Jiǎnpǔ) — Number Notation

```
1  2  3  4  5  6  7
do re mi fa sol la ti
```

With dots above/below for octave, lines for rhythm. This maps directly to Western diatonic — it's essentially the same system with different symbols. Not interesting for constraint theory.

### 4.2 工尺谱 (Gōngchěpǔ) — Traditional Character Notation

Uses characters to indicate relative pitch in a heptatonic scale:

```
合 四 一 上 尺 工 凡 六 五 乙
(sometimes read as solfege-like: do re mi fa sol la ti)
```

More interesting: **工尺谱 is a relative notation**. The same characters apply regardless of the absolute pitch of the fundamental. It encodes **scale degree** not pitch — a constraint, not a value.

This is exactly what `constraint_theory_core` does with the A₂ lattice: positions are relative to the lattice, not absolute. The covering radius guarantees bounded error *regardless of where you start*.

### 4.3 减字谱 (Jiǎnzìpǔ) — Guqin Tablature

**This is the most radical notation system for constraint theory.**

Jiǎnzìpǔ does not encode:
- Absolute pitch
- Relative pitch
- Rhythm
- Duration

It encodes:
- Which string to play
- Which finger to use
- Where to place the finger (position on string)
- How to pluck (direction, finger)
- Which finger techniques to apply (vibrato, glissando, bend)

The musician must resolve ALL musical parameters from these physical constraints. This is the **deadband funnel in its purest form**:

```
Notation (constraint set) → [Musician's interpretation] → Sound
                                ↑
                          This is the funnel:
                          - Multiple valid solutions
                          - Convergence toward a target region
                          - Error bounded by musician's skill
```

**Jiǎnzìpǔ as a PLATO tile:** A piece written in jiǎnzìpǔ is a constraint specification, not a score. Different musicians will produce different realizations, all valid. This is *exactly* what a StyleTile does when it specifies deadbands rather than exact values — it defines the acceptable region, not the exact point.

## 5. Building a Chinese Music Constraint Theory

### 5.1 PentatonicLattice — A New Lattice Type

```python
from dataclasses import dataclass
from typing import Tuple, List
import math

@dataclass(frozen=True)
class PentatonicPoint:
    """A point in the pentatonic constraint space.
    
    Coordinates are in the wǔxíng (five-phase) system:
    - gōng (Earth): 0
    - shāng (Metal): 1  
    - jué (Wood): 2
    - zhǐ (Fire): 3
    - yǔ (Water): 4
    
    The fifth-chain maps these to semitones: {0, 2, 4, 7, 9}
    """
    phase: int  # 0-4, index into the pentatonic
    octave: int
    
    def to_semitone(self) -> int:
        SEMITONES = [0, 2, 4, 7, 9]
        return SEMITONES[self.phase % 5] + self.octave * 12
    
    def xiāngshēng(self) -> 'PentatonicPoint':
        """Next tone in the generating cycle."""
        return PentatonicPoint((self.phase + 1) % 5, self.octave)
    
    def xiāngkè(self) -> 'PentatonicPoint':
        """Next tone in the overcoming cycle."""
        return PentatonicPoint((self.phase + 2) % 5, self.octave)


@dataclass(frozen=True)  
class LǜPoint:
    """A point in the 十二律 Pythagorean spiral.
    
    Unlike A₂ which uses equal temperament, this uses
    exact Pythagorean ratios from 三分损益.
    """
    lǜ_index: int  # 0-11 (which of the 12 pitches)
    octave: int
    
    # The 12 Pythagorean ratios (as fractions)
    RATIOS = [
        (1, 1),       # 黄钟 Huángzhōng
        (2187, 2048), # 大吕 Dàlǚ
        (9, 8),       # 太簇 Tàicù
        (19683, 16384), # 夹钟 Jiāzhōng
        (81, 64),     # 姑洗 Gūxǐ
        (177147, 131072), # 仲吕 Zhònglǚ
        (729, 512),   # 蕤宾 Ruíbīn
        (3, 2),       # 林钟 Línzhōng
        (6561, 4096), # 夷则 Yízé
        (27, 16),     # 南吕 Nánlǚ
        (59049, 32768), # 无射 Wúyì
        (243, 128),   # 应钟 Yìngzhōng
    ]
    
    def frequency(self, fundamental: float = 261.63) -> float:
        """Compute the exact frequency using Pythagorean ratios."""
        ratio_num, ratio_den = self.RATIOS[self.lǜ_index % 12]
        return fundamental * (ratio_num / ratio_den) * (2 ** self.octave)
    
    def to_cents(self) -> float:
        """Distance from fundamental in cents."""
        ratio_num, ratio_den = self.RATIOS[self.lǜ_index % 12]
        return 1200 * math.log2(ratio_num / ratio_den) + self.octave * 1200


def pentatonic_snap(x: float, y: float) -> Tuple[PentatonicPoint, float]:
    """Snap a continuous pitch to the nearest pentatonic tone.
    
    The pentatonic lattice is sparser than A₂, so the covering 
    radius is larger: ρ₅ ≈ 0.894 semitones (vs 0.577 for A₂).
    
    This means pentatonic snap is MORE FORGIVING — notes can 
    drift further before they're "wrong."
    """
    SEMITONES = [0, 2, 4, 7, 9]
    
    # Convert (x, y) in Eisenstein space to semitone
    semitone = x  # simplified: x-axis = semitone axis
    
    # Find nearest semitone in pentatonic
    best_idx = 0
    best_dist = float('inf')
    for i, s in enumerate(SEMITONES):
        for oct_shift in [-1, 0, 1]:
            dist = abs(semitone - (s + oct_shift * 12))
            if dist < best_dist:
                best_dist = dist
                best_idx = i
                best_oct = oct_shift
    
    return PentatonicPoint(best_idx, best_oct), best_dist
```

### 5.2 Xuángōng (旋宫) — Modulation as Rotation

旋宫 literally means "rotating the palace" — shifting the fundamental pitch through the 12 lǜ while maintaining the pentatonic interval structure.

**Mathematically:** This is a **group action** of Z/12Z on the pentatonic set:

```
Tₖ: S₅ → S₅ + k (mod 12)
```

where S₅ = {0, 2, 4, 7, 9} and addition is mod 12.

The 12 transpositions produce 12 pentatonic scales, but some are **duplicates** (because 5 and 12 share no common factor). In fact, all 12 transpositions are distinct — there are exactly 12 unique pentatonic modes in 12-TET.

In constraint-theory terms, 旋宫 is **rotating the entire constraint lattice** while preserving its internal structure. The deadbands don't change; only the origin moves.

### 5.3 Yùn (韵) — Resonance as a Constraint

韵 (yùn) is one of the most important concepts in Chinese aesthetics. It refers to the **resonant afterlife** of a sound — the timbral, emotional, and spatial qualities that persist after the initial attack.

**As a constraint:** Yùn constrains the *space between notes* rather than the notes themselves. It's a constraint on **absence** — what happens when nothing is playing.

In constraint-theory terms, this is a constraint on the **deadband funnel's shape** during note decay:

```python
@dataclass
class YùnConstraint:
    """Constraints on the resonant afterlife of a tone."""
    decay_time: float        # seconds until silence
    decay_shape: str         # "exponential", "linear", "sustained"
    spectral_drift: float    # how much the harmonic content shifts during decay
    pitch_drift: float       # cents of pitch bend during decay (guqin: up to ±50¢)
    min_audibility: float    # dB below which we consider the note "gone"
```

This has no direct Western equivalent. Western constraint theory focuses on the *presence* of notes (pitch, timing, harmony). Yùn constrains their *absence* — the decay, the silence, the space between.

### 5.4 Liúbái (留白) — Leaving White Space

留白 literally means "leaving white" — from Chinese painting tradition where the unpainted areas are as important as the painted ones.

**As a musical constraint:** Silence is not the absence of music; it's an active structural element.

```python
@dataclass
class LiúbáiConstraint:
    """Constraints on intentional silence."""
    min_rest_duration: float    # minimum silence to count as liúbái (beats)
    max_note_density: float     # maximum notes per beat in a phrase
    silence_ratio: float        # target fraction of silence in a section
    breathing_points: List[float]  # positions in a phrase where rest is required
```

In constraint-theory terms, liúbái is a **lower bound on the deadband funnel's width** — you MUST have empty space, not just allowed to. It's a minimum-distance constraint in time: consecutive notes cannot be too close.

## 6. Cross-Cultural Synthesis

### 6.1 Eisenstein Lattice + 十二律 Pythagorean Spiral

These are NOT the same lattice, but they're related:

| Property | A₂ Eisenstein | 十二律 Pythagorean |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Basis | {1, ω} in C | {3/2, 2/1} in R⁺ |
| Space | Z² → C (2D complex) | Z² → R⁺ (log-frequency) |
| Covering radius | 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 st | ~0.467 st (12-tone) |
| Closure | Closed (12-TET) | Open (spiral) |
| Precision | Fixed (equal) | Tunable (extend spiral) |
| Error | Bounded by construction | Bounded per N tones |

**Synthesis:** Use the A₂ lattice for **rhythmic** and **voice-leading** constraints (where equal spacing is desired), and the 三分损益 spiral for **pitch** constraints (where just intonation is preferred).

```python
@dataclass
class HybridLattice:
    """A constraint lattice combining A₂ (rhythm) and Pythagorean (pitch)."""
    
    # Rhythmic constraints: A₂ Eisenstein lattice
    rhythmic_snap = snap  # from constraint_theory_core.lattice
    
    # Pitch constraints: Pythagorean spiral
    pitch_snap = lǜ_snap  # from the 三分损益 system
    
    def hybrid_snap(self, time: float, freq: float) -> Tuple:
        """Snap (time, frequency) to the hybrid lattice."""
        # Time dimension: A₂ snap (equal divisions)
        rhythmic_point, time_error = self.rhythmic_snap(time, 0)
        
        # Frequency dimension: Pythagorean snap (just ratios)
        pitch_point, freq_error = self.pitch_snap(freq)
        
        return (rhythmic_point, pitch_point), (time_error, freq_error)
```

### 6.2 Deadband Funnel + 气韵 (Qìyùn)

气韵 (qìyùn) — literally "breath-resonance" — is the Chinese concept of rhythmic flow. It's related to but different from Western "groove":

| Concept | Western Groove | Chinese Qìyùn |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| Organization | Metric grid | Breath cycle |
| Timing | Deadband around grid points | Deadband around breath points |
| Measure | Beats per minute | Phrases per breath |
| Syncopation | Displacement against grid | Displacement against breath |
| Feel | "In the pocket" | "行云流水" (flowing like clouds and water) |

**Synthesis:** Use deadband funnels for **grid-based** music (most Western genres) and breath-cycle funnels for **flow-based** music (Chinese traditional, ambient):

```python
@dataclass
class BreathCycleFunnel:
    """A deadband funnel organized around breathing cycles, not metric grids."""
    breath_length: float       # average seconds per breath cycle
    inhale_ratio: float        # fraction of cycle that's "active" (inhale = notes)
    exhale_ratio: float        # fraction that's "release" (exhale = decay/silence)
    pause_ratio: float         # fraction that's rest (between breaths)
    
    # These map to the deadband funnel's three phases:
    # inhale → CONVERGENCE (notes settle into target)
    # exhale → STABILITY (notes hold at target, then drift)  
    # pause → DIVERGENCE (notes release, liúbái/silence)
    
    def snap(self, time: float, phase: float) -> Tuple[float, float]:
        """Snap a time point to the breath cycle.
        
        Unlike A₂ snap which maps to a fixed grid, this maps to 
        a breathing rhythm that can stretch and compress.
        """
        cycle_pos = (time % self.breath_length) / self.breath_length
        # The "target" is the boundary between inhale and exhale
        target = self.inhale_ratio
        error = abs(cycle_pos - target)
        return cycle_pos, error
```

### 6.3 Laman Rigidity + 对位 (Heterophony)

Western counterpoint: multiple independent voices, each following its own melodic line, with **harmonic** constraints between them.

Chinese heterophony (支声复调): multiple instruments play the **same** melody simultaneously, but each ornaments it differently. The constraint is that they must be recognizable as the same melody, not that they must form valid harmony.

**In Laman rigidity terms:**

- **Western counterpoint:** N vertices (voices) with constraints on **edges** (harmonic intervals). Rigid when 2N-3 edges satisfy Laman condition.
- **Chinese heterophony:** N vertices (instruments) with constraints on **distance from a shared skeleton** (the core melody). Each vertex must stay within a deadband of the skeleton.

These are structurally different:
- Counterpoint: `rigidity(edges)` — the GRAPH is rigid
- Heterophony: `rigidity(vertex_to_center)` — each VERTEX is bounded

**Synthesis:** A combined system that allows both:

```python
@dataclass  
class CulturalRigidity:
    """Rigidity constraints that support both counterpoint and heterophony."""
    mode: str  # "counterpoint", "heterophony", or "hybrid"
    
    # Counterpoint parameters (Western)
    n_voices: int
    max_parallel: int  # max consecutive parallel intervals
    min_independence: float  # minimum voice independence score
    
    # Heterophony parameters (Chinese)
    skeleton_melody: List[float]  # the core melody (in semitones)
    ornamentation_deadband: float  # max deviation from skeleton
    ornamentation_style: str  # "grace_notes", "bends", "glissandi"
    
    # Hybrid: how much of each
    counterpoint_weight: float  # 0.0 = pure heterophony, 1.0 = pure counterpoint
```

### 6.4 A Unified System

3. **Voice structure:** Uses Laman rigidity for counterpoint AND heterophony, with adjustable weights
4. **Timbre space:** Uses harmonic series (guqin huī positions) for just-intonation snap, with A₂ for equal-temperament snap
5. **Silence space:** Uses liúbái constraints to enforce minimum silence, and yùn constraints to shape note decay
6. **Notation space:** Uses jiǎnzìpǔ-style action-constraints (what to do) alongside Western pitch-constraints (what to hear)

### 7.1 Lattice Abstraction

Currently `COVERING_RADIUS` is a constant (1/√3). For non-uniform lattices (just intonation, Pythagorean), it needs to be a function of position:

### 7.3 Direction Sets

The `DODECET_DIRECTIONS` (12 nearest neighbors) are specific to A₂. Pentatonic systems need only 5 or 10 direction vectors, and Pythagorean systems need directional vectors based on just-intonation ratios.

### 7.4 Snap Target Flexibility

`snap()` currently returns an `A2Point`. It should return a generic `LatticePoint` that can represent:

- Pentatonic phase + octave
- Pythagorean lǜ index + octave

### 7.5 New Constraint Types

The current system handles:
- **Distance constraints** (snap error < covering radius)
- **Holonomy constraints** (direction cycles sum to zero)
- **Rigidity constraints** (Laman condition on voice graphs)

Chinese music adds:
- **Phase constraints** (wǔxíng generating/overcoming cycles)
- **Silence constraints** (liúbái minimum silence)
- **Decay constraints** (yùn resonance parameters)
- **Breath constraints** (qìyùn flow patterns)
- **Action constraints** (jiǎnzìpǔ — what to do, not what to hear)

## 8. Key Insights and Discoveries

### 8.1 The Pentatonic IS the Circle of Fifths (Truncated)

The wǔxíng generating cycle (相生) is not metaphorically but **mathematically identical** to the circle of fifths, truncated at 5 elements. This gives the pentatonic a clean Z/5Z structure in fifth-space that's more elegant than its messy embedding in Z/12Z.

### 8.2 The Wǔxíng System is Laman-Rigid with Redundancy

The two five-phase cycles (generating + overcoming) create a 10-edge graph on 5 vertices that is over-constrained by 3 edges. This provides mathematical self-repair: losing up to 3 constraints doesn't collapse the system. **Chinese philosophy encodes a fault-tolerant constraint system.**

### 8.3 三分损益 Has a Tighter Covering Radius Than A₂

The Pythagorean spiral (12 tones) has ρ ≈ 0.467 semitones, compared to A₂'s ρ ≈ 0.577. The Chinese system is *more precise* for pitch snapping. However, it pays for this by being an open spiral (no closure), while A₂ is a closed group.

### 8.4 Jiǎnzìpǔ is Pure Constraint Theory

Guqin tablature encodes actions, not pitches. The musician is a real-time constraint solver. This is the deadband funnel in its purest form: notation = constraints, performance = convergence.

### 8.5 Liúbái is a Minimum-Silence Constraint

Western constraint theory focuses on notes (presence). Chinese music theory constrains silence (absence). Liúbái is a minimum-distance constraint in time — you MUST have empty space.

### 8.6 The Guqin Lattice is Non-Uniform

Unlike the A₂ lattice where every point has the same neighborhood, the guqin's harmonic positions create a non-uniform lattice where simple ratios (octave, fifth) are "close" (tight constraints) and complex ratios (tritone) are "far" (loose constraints). This is more musically expressive.

### 8.7 The Synthesis is Achievable

A unified system that supports both Western (A₂, equal temperament, counterpoint) and Chinese (三分损益, pentatonic, heterophony) music is architecturally feasible. The key changes are:
1. Abstract the lattice (currently hard-coded to A₂)
2. Add non-uniform covering radii
3. Add silence/decay constraint types
4. Support action-based notation (constraints on what to do)
