## What Each Paradigm Got Right and Wrong

**Constraint Theory (1-18):** Right that constraints shape creativity. Wrong that the constraint IS the creativity. The lattice is the stage, not the play.

**Attractor Theory (19-50):** Right that the Lorenz attractor IS the creative dynamics. Wrong that quality peaks at ρ=47 — that was just variance. Right that coupling hurts individual quality. Wrong that it hurts everything.

**Metric Theory (51-60):** Right that the quality metric was confounded. Right that Fisher Information and CE are genuine. Wrong that either alone captures creativity. Right that truth lives in disagreement.

**Structure Theory (61-76):** Right that Fisher peaks at the phase transition. Right that there are 5+ species. Right that creativity is multi-objective. Still vulnerable to the next falsification.

## The Three Deepest Insights

1. **The attractor IS the music** — but "is" means "has the same shape as," not "sounds like." The mapping from attractor to sound is a choice, not a given. The same attractor maps to A# with circle-of-fifths and to Bhairavi with microtonal mapping. The attractor provides dynamics; the mapping provides meaning.

2. **Creativity IS variance — and that's not a problem** — the confound isn't a bug. Creativity IS the production of statistical improbability. The question isn't "how to measure creativity without variance" but "which variance is creative and which is noise?" Fisher Information answers this: creative variance is the variance that is maximally sensitive to parameter changes.

3. **The session IS the instrument** — not the Lorenz system, not the code, not the parameters. The session — the trajectory through experiment space — is what produces insight. Individual experiments are notes. The session is the symphony. And the conductor's job is to optimize the trajectory, not any individual note.
